Prevention Tips and Advice

  • Clean hands frequently, either with an alcohol-based hand sanitizer, or with soap and water.
  • When washing hands with soap and water, scrub your hands for at least 20 seconds as recommended by The CDC, which is typically the amount of time it takes to sing “Happy Birthday” twice.
  • To turn off the tap, use a disposable towel or other disposable object, and make sure not to touch the tap.

  • When coughing and sneezing, cover your mouth with your elbow to prevent spread.

  • Avoid close contact with others, especially with those coughing or sneezing
  • At least 1 meter (3 feet) distance away from others is recommended by The WHO.

  • Avoid touching your face, particularly your nose, eyes, and mouth before washing hands thoroughly.

  • If you decide to wear a mask, do not touch the front of the mask, and make sure to discard it by holding it by the elastics on the side.
  • Do not use disposable masks twice, and wash your hands before putting on a mask.
  • Masks should cover your nose, mouth, and chin.
  • Masks are only effective when used properly as detailed above, otherwise they could help spread the virus.
